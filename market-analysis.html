📈 SaaS MARKET ANALYSIS - June 18, 2026

Market Overview
•The global Software as a Service (SaaS) market size in 2026 is estimated at USD 435.41 billion, growing from the 2025 value of USD 370.4 billion, with 2031 projections reaching USD 976.61 billion at a 17.55% CAGR [1].
•Expansion is driven by enterprise cloud migration, rapid AI integration, and the attractive economics of pay-as-you-go delivery models [1].
•North America remains the largest market, while Asia Pacific is the fastest-growing region [1]; healthcare is emerging as a high-growth vertical with a 22.9% CAGR due to digital health and compliance needs [1].
•Worldwide SaaS revenue is expected to grow at an annual rate of 19.38% between 2025–2029, reaching $793.10 billion by 2029 [2].
•End-user spending on all public cloud services is projected to surpass $1 trillion by 2027 [2].

Investment & Funding Trends
•AI-native SaaS applications saw spending increase 108% year-over-year, making them the hottest category attracting capital [6].
•The global AI-created SaaS market (SaaS products powered by AI) is estimated to reach $770.32 billion by 2031, growing at a 40.2% CAGR from 2024–2031 [2].
•Usage-based pricing is a key trend, with Gartner projecting 70% of top SaaS vendors will offer consumption-based pricing for at least part of their portfolio by 2027 [6].
•Organizations spend an average of $55.7 million annually on SaaS, managing roughly 305 applications per company [6].
•Valuation multiples remain strong for AI-integrated and high-retention SaaS companies, though renewal volatility is a growing concern for investors [6].

Industry Performance
•Leading public SaaS companies include Salesforce, Oracle Corp., SAP SE, Rackspace Technology Inc., and VMware Inc. [4].
•North America dominated the global market with 46% share in 2025, driven by large enterprises accounting for 62% of revenue [5].
•The public cloud segment is expected to lead the market, contributing 65.81% globally in 2026, particularly in BFSI sectors [7].
•Software is projected to be the fastest-growing IT spending category in 2026, with Gartner forecasting software spending to grow 15.2% year-over-year [6].
•SaaS application counts declined slightly by 0.07% year-over-year, signaling stabilization rather than continued sprawl [6].
