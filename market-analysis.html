📈 SaaS MARKET ANALYSIS - July 02, 2026

Market Overview
The global SaaS market is forecast to reach $465.03 billion in 2026, representing a significant year-over-year increase from 2025’s $408.21 billion[1][6]. Growth projections indicate the market will expand at a CAGR of 12.85% from 2026 to 2035, reaching approximately $1.37 trillion by 2035[1]. An alternative forecast suggests a higher CAGR of 17.55% through 2031, reaching $976.61 billion[2]. Key trends driving the industry include AI-native application adoption, rapid growth in usage-based pricing models, and rising renewal volatility[6]. The industry is heavily influenced by digital transformation and cloud adoption, accelerating demand for work-from-anywhere tools[1]. North America remains the largest market with 46% of global share in 2025, while Asia-Pacific is the fastest-growing region with a projected 18.7% CAGR[1][2]. Large enterprises account for 62% of revenue, though SMEs are the fastest-growing cohort at 19.2% CAGR[1][2].

Investment & Funding Trends
Investment interest remains high despite broader economic fluctuations, with strategic capital flowing heavily into AI-created SaaS and Security & Compliance applications[2][6]. Spending on AI-native SaaS applications increased 108% year-over-year, signaling a massive shift in capital allocation[6]. The Global AI SaaS market is expected to grow at a 38.28% CAGR, reaching $775.44 billion by 2031[5]. Security and Compliance applications are advancing at a 24.1% CAGR, making them a hot category for investors[2]. While specific average valuation multiples by stage vary by sector, the market favors subscription plans (68.2% of market) which are transitioning toward usage-based models (rising at 27.9% CAGR)[2]. Healthcare is the fastest-growing end-user vertical at 22.9% CAGR, attracting significant venture capital[2].

Industry Performance
Public SaaS companies continue to see revenue growth, though markets face renewal volatility and increased overlap between SaaS management and FinOps practices[6]. The industry foundation remains strong with revenue climbing and regional markets scaling rapidly[6]. A notable pipeline trend is the shift toward consumption-based pricing; Gartner predicts that by 2027, 70% of top SaaS vendors will offer this model for at least part of their portfolio[6]. IT and Telecom remains the leading end-user vertical with 22.45% market share, while Healthcare is the fastest-growing sector[2]. Software is projected to be the fastest-growing IT spending category in 2026, with global software spending forecast to reach $1.43 trillion[6].
