📈 SaaS MARKET ANALYSIS - June 20, 2026

Market Overview
•Current SaaS market size and growth projections: The global SaaS market is estimated at USD 465.03 billion in 2026, representing a significant year-over-year increase from USD 408.21 billion in 2025 [1]. Forecasts project the market to expand to approximately USD 1.37 trillion by 2035, growing at a CAGR of 12.85% from 2026 to 2035 [1]. Some models indicate a faster trajectory, with a projected CAGR of 18.7% through 2034, reaching USD 1.48 trillion [6].
•Key trends driving the industry: The primary growth driver is the rapid adoption of AI-native SaaS applications, with spending on these tools surging 108% year-over-year [5]. Usage-based pricing models are replacing traditional subscription fees, while renewal volatility is increasing due to economic scrutiny [5]. Additionally, 60%+ of enterprise SaaS products now have embedded AI features, fundamentally reshaping product capabilities [4].
•Geographic market distribution: North America dominates the global market with a 46.9% share in 2025, driven by early cloud adoption and large enterprise density [1]. Asia-Pacific is the fastest-growing region, projected to expand rapidly due to digital transformation in emerging economies [2]. The Healthcare sector is a standout geographic and vertical driver, anticipating a 23.37% market share in 2026 and the highest CAGR of 26.00% [6].

•Recent funding rounds and investment patterns: Investment interest remains high despite broader economic fluctuations, with funding in the Agentic AI sector surging 143% year-over-year in Q1 2026 [9]. The average funding round size has doubled to $155 million, signaling a shift toward larger, more mature capital injections for high-growth categories [9]. Strategic investment remains focused on AI-integrated platforms that solve specific enterprise inefficiencies [5].

The global AI-created SaaS market is estimated to reach $770.32 billion by 2031, growing at a CAGR of 40.2% [2]. Healthcare SaaS and Content, Collaboration & Communication tools (29.19% market share) are also attracting significant capital [6].
•Average valuation multiples by company stage: While specific 2026 multiples vary by niche, the market is seeing a consolidation of valuation logic where AI-native companies command significantly higher multiples than traditional SaaS. Companies with embedded AI features and usage-based pricing models are seeing valuation premiums due to their higher growth rates and sticky revenue models [4].
