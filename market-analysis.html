📈 SaaS MARKET ANALYSIS - June 13, 2026

Market Overview
•The global SaaS market is estimated between $255.8B and $465.03B in 2026, depending on methodology and scope; multiple forecasts place 2026 growth in the 10%–19% CAGR range through the early 2030s.[2][3][5][7]
•A more conservative forecast from Straits Research pegs the market at $255.8B in 2026 and $563.66B by 2034 (10.38% CAGR), while Fortune Business Insights projects $375.57B in 2026 and $1.48T by 2034 (18.7% CAGR).[2][3]
•Vertical SaaS is expanding faster than the broad market: one forecast estimates $143.45B in 2026 growing to $499.42B by 2035 (16.3% CAGR).[1]
•North America remains the largest SaaS region in most forecasts, with shares reported around 46.9%–52%; Asia-Pacific is typically the fastest-growing region, with forecast growth around 12.1%+ annually in some estimates.[1][2][3][4]
•Key growth drivers include AI-native SaaS, multi-cloud/hybrid-cloud demand, SME digitization, and growing enterprise focus on integration, security, and ease of use.[3][4][6][7]
•SaaS usage is also consolidating: Zylo reports the average company manages 305 SaaS applications, while another benchmark cites 106 apps per company in 2024, with application sprawl stabilizing rather than exploding.[6][7]

Investment & Funding Trends
•The strongest capital is flowing into AI-native SaaS, especially products with clear workflow automation, vertical specialization, and measurable ROI; one 2026 trend report says agentic AI funding surged 143% YoY in Q1 2026 and average round sizes doubled to $155M.[8]
•Broadly, the market is favoring categories tied to automation, security, data infrastructure, developer tools, compliance, and vertical workflows, because these areas map directly to cost reduction or revenue lift. This is an inference from the funding pattern emphasized in the sources, not a single quoted dataset.[6][7][8]
•Usage-based and consumption-based pricing is becoming more common: Zylo reports Gartner expects 70% of top SaaS vendors to offer consumption-based pricing for at least part of their portfolio by 2027.[7]
•Valuation multiples vary widely by growth, margin, and category, but the current market clearly rewards companies with AI adoption, efficient growth, and retention strength; the provided results do not include a reliable cross-stage median EV/revenue multiple dataset, so any exact multiple would be speculative from these sources alone.[6][7][8]
