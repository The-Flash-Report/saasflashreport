📈 SaaS MARKET ANALYSIS - June 22, 2026

Market Overview
•The SaaS market is still expanding at scale, but reputable estimates vary widely: Mordor Intelligence puts the 2026 market at USD 435.41 billion with 17.55% CAGR to 2031, while Grand View Research estimates USD 464.8 billion in 2026 and 12.0% CAGR to 2030.[2][9]
•More conservative research firms are lower: Research Nester estimates USD 372.41 billion in 2026 for enterprise SaaS applications and USD 2.03 trillion by 2035, while Straits Research places the market at USD 255.8 billion in 2026.[1][4]
•The dispersion in estimates reflects different definitions of “SaaS” and scope differences across enterprise applications, broader SaaS, and adjacent cloud software categories.[1][2][4][9]
•The strongest demand drivers are enterprise cloud migration, AI integration, usage-based pricing, and security/compliance needs.[2][6]
•North America remains the largest regional market in multiple reports, with Mordor citing 42.60% share in 2025, while Asia-Pacific is the fastest-growing region in several forecasts.[2][4]
•Vertical demand is led by IT and Telecom at 22.45% of the SaaS market in 2025, while Healthcare is one of the fastest-growing verticals at 22.9% CAGR through 2031.[2]

Investment & Funding Trends
•Capital continues to favor SaaS businesses with clear AI leverage, workflow automation, and strong retention economics; industry trackers report a meaningful shift toward AI-native SaaS and consumption-based pricing.[5][6]
•Zylo reports that spending on AI-native SaaS applications increased 108% year over year, and Gartner expects 70% of top SaaS vendors to offer some consumption-based pricing by 2027.[6]
•BetterCloud notes that the average company now manages 305 SaaS applications, while the average number of SaaS apps per company was 106 in 2024, indicating that tool sprawl is stabilizing even as spend grows.[5][6]
•The most attractive SaaS categories for capital are AI infrastructure and workflow SaaS, security/compliance, vertical SaaS, FinOps/SaaS management, and healthcare software, based on the fastest-growing segments cited in market reports and usage trends.[2][5][6]
