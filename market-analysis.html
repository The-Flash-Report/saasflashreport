📈 SaaS MARKET ANALYSIS - July 05, 2026

Market Overview
•Current SaaS market size and growth projections: The global B2B SaaS market is valued at USD 0.49 trillion in 2026, expanding at a CAGR of 26.24% to reach USD 1.58 trillion by 2031[1]. The broader global SaaS market is forecast to grow from USD 465.03 billion in 2026 to approximately USD 1.37 trillion by 2035 at a CAGR of 12.85%[3].
•Key trends driving the industry: The market is heavily influenced by the rapid adoption of AI-native SaaS applications, which saw spending increase 108% year-over-year in 2026[6]. Other critical trends include the shift to usage-based pricing models (with 70% of top vendors expected to offer consumption-based pricing by 2027) and rising renewal volatility[6]. Investors now prioritize "efficient growth" centered on the Rule of 40 (combining revenue growth and profit margin) over growth-at-all-costs[9].
•Geographic market distribution: North America remains the largest market, retaining a 32.85% revenue share (2025 data) and dominating with 46.9% of the global market share in 2025[1][4]. Asia Pacific is the fastest-growing region, posting a regional CAGR of 24.60% and identified as the "Fastest Growing Market" for B2B SaaS[1].

Investment & Funding Trends
•Recent funding rounds and investment patterns: 2025 set records for SaaS deal volume with 17 mega-deals exceeding $2.5 billion and total announced value surpassing $180 billion; 2026 is shaping up to be even larger[5]. The industry is undergoing its most significant transformation since cloud computing, with a structural repricing of valuations rather than a panic reversal[5].
•Hot SaaS categories attracting capital: Vertical SaaS and Cybersecurity are the standout high-growth areas for 2026[9]. The Global AI SaaS market is projected to grow at a massive 38.28% CAGR, rising from $71.54 billion in 2023 to $775.44 billion by 2031[2].
•Average valuation multiples by company stage: Public SaaS revenue multiples are stabilizing in the 5.0x–6.5x range through the rest of 2026, with a wide bifurcation between AI-native leaders (10x+) and vulnerable horizontal point-solutions (2–4x)[5]. Private venture deal multiples are stabilizing at 4.2x–5.2x, while private M&A multiples are at 3.8x–4.8x[5].

Industry Performance
•Public SaaS company stock performance: Public SaaS median multiples dropped from 6.1x in Q3 2025 to 5.1x–5.5x by March 2026 due to "AI disruption fears" exploding in Q1 2026[5]. The bottom is likely near, with stabilization expected in the 5.0x–6.0x range[5].
