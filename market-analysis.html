📈 SaaS MARKET ANALYSIS - June 02, 2026

Market Overview
•The global SaaS market is forecast to reach $465.03 billion in 2026, up from $408.21 billion in 2025, according to one 2026 market estimate, while another projects the market at $375.57 billion in 2026 with growth to $1.48 trillion by 2034; the gap reflects differing methodology and segment scope across analysts.[2][3]
•Broader medium-term projections remain consistently strong: estimates range from $819.23 billion by 2030 to $1.37 trillion by 2035, with CAGRs between 12.0% and 18.7% depending on the source.[8][2][3]
•AI is now a major demand driver: one 2026 SaaS statistics set says AI-native SaaS spending rose 108% year over year, and AI-created SaaS is projected to grow from $71.54 billion in 2023 to $775.44 billion by 2031.[7][6]
•Software budgets are still expanding even as app sprawl stabilizes; Zylo reports an average of 305 SaaS applications per company and $55.7 million in annual SaaS spend, while BetterCloud notes the average number of SaaS apps per company fell to 106 in 2024, with consolidation slowing.[7][6]
•Geographic concentration remains high: North America leads with roughly 46%–46.9% market share in broad SaaS estimates, while Europe is also a major second market; vertical SaaS research suggests North America alone holds 48%–52% of that segment.[2][3][1]

Investment & Funding Trends
•Capital is still flowing toward AI-enabled workflow software, vertical SaaS, security, data infrastructure, and finance/operations tooling, with the strongest investor interest going to products that show clear ROI and pricing power; this pattern is consistent with the growth in AI-native SaaS adoption and software spending cited by Zylo and BetterCloud.[7][6]
•The hottest categories are increasingly vertical SaaS and AI-native SaaS rather than horizontal point tools, because vertical solutions show clearer expansion paths and stronger willingness to pay; the vertical SaaS market is estimated at $143.45 billion in 2026 and projected to reach $499.42 billion by 2035.[1]
•Public-market and private-market multiples remain sensitive to growth quality, but the recent source set does not provide a single reliable cross-stage valuation benchmark for June 2026; any precise multiple would require live comp data from transaction databases or bank research not included here.
•A practical investor takeaway is that efficient growth now matters more than raw revenue growth: software spending is still rising, but buyers are scrutinizing consolidation, renewal risk, and AI return on investment more closely.[7][6]
