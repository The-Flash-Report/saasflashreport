📈 SaaS MARKET ANALYSIS - July 04, 2026

Market Overview
•Current SaaS market size and growth projections: The global SaaS market reached $492.34 billion in 2026, representing a 26% year-over-year increase from $390 billion in 2025[1]. The B2B SaaS segment is projected to grow at a 26.24% CAGR, reaching $1.58 trillion by 2031[1]. Enterprise SaaS revenues grew 31% year-over-year, hitting nearly $15 billion in quarterly revenues[1].
•Key trends driving the industry: AI-native SaaS is the dominant growth category, with spending on these applications increasing 108% year-over-year[7]. The AI-created SaaS market is expected to reach $142.02 billion in 2026, growing at a 39.6% CAGR to $1.05 trillion by 2033[1]. Vertical SaaS is outpacing horizontal growth at 45% annually[1], while usage-based pricing now accounts for 35% of new contracts[1].
•Geographic market distribution: North America dominated the global market with a 46.9% share in 2025[3]. Approximately 75% of apps are now SaaS, with only 25% remaining on-prem in 2025[2].

Investment & Funding Trends
•Recent funding rounds and investment patterns: AI-native SaaS companies are raising at 40% higher valuations than traditional SaaS[1]. Strategic investment interest remains high despite broader economic fluctuations, with 2025 being a record year for SaaS M&A and 2026 shaping up to be even bigger[5]. Private SaaS multiples have proven more resilient than public, relying on fundamental metrics like ARR growth and NRR rather than sentiment[5].
•Hot SaaS categories attracting capital: AI-native SaaS and Vertical SaaS command the most premium valuations[8]. Cybersecurity is another standout sector offering outsized growth opportunities[8]. Companies achieving $100M ARR in 18 months are the primary targets for high-valuation capital[1].
•Average valuation multiples by company stage: Public SaaS revenue multiples are stabilizing in the 5.0x–6.5x range for 2026, with AI-native leaders reaching 10x+ and vulnerable horizontal point-solutions at 2–4x[5]. Private SaaS venture deal multiples are stabilizing at 4.2x–5.2x, while private SaaS M&A multiples are at 3.8x–4.8x[5].
