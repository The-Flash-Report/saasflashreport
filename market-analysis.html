📈 SaaS MARKET ANALYSIS - June 15, 2026

Market Overview
•The global SaaS market is estimated at $255.8B in 2026 in one major market forecast, with a projected rise to $563.66B by 2034 at a 10.38% CAGR.[2]
•Other 2026 forecasts are materially higher: one projects the market at $375.57B in 2026 rising to $1.48T by 2034 (18.7% CAGR), while another places 2026 SaaS revenue at $465.03B.[3][7]
•The spread across estimates reflects different market definitions and scope, but all point to strong double-digit growth driven by cloud migration, enterprise software modernization, and AI adoption.[2][3][7]
•Vertical SaaS is one of the fastest-growing subsegments, estimated at $143.45B in 2026 and projected to reach $499.42B by 2035 (16.3% CAGR).[1]
•North America remains the largest regional market in several forecasts, with shares around 46.9%–52% depending on scope, while Asia-Pacific is repeatedly identified as the fastest-growing region.[1][2][3][7]
•Key demand drivers include AI-native software, usage-based pricing, rising SaaS spend governance, and enterprise demand for integration and security.[4][6][7]

Investment & Funding Trends
•Search results indicate that AI-native and AI-created SaaS are attracting the most attention, with one source citing an AI Created SaaS market reaching $770.32B by 2031 at 40.2% CAGR.[4]
•Another source reports AI SaaS expected to grow from $71.54B in 2023 to $775.44B by 2031 at 38.28% CAGR, underscoring strong investor interest in AI software layers.[6]
•The clearest capital concentration is in agentic AI, where one 2026 trend report says the market is $11.8B in 2026, funding was up 143% YoY in Q1 2026, and average round size doubled to $155M.[8]
•The hottest SaaS categories appear to be AI infrastructure, workflow automation, security, FinOps/SaaS management, and vertical SaaS for regulated industries, based on the dominance of AI and governance trends in the available sources.[1][4][6][7][8]
