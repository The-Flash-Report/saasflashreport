📈 SaaS MARKET ANALYSIS - June 24, 2026

Market Overview
•Current SaaS market size and growth projections: The global SaaS market reached approximately $375–$465 billion in early 2026, with enterprise software spending rising 14.7% year-over-year according to Gartner [1]. Specific B2B forecasts value the market at $492.34 billion in 2026, projecting a 26.24% CAGR to reach $1.58 trillion by 2031 [2]. Other models suggest a larger trajectory, estimating growth from $375.57 billion (2026) to $1.48 trillion by 2034 at an 18.7% CAGR [5].
•Key trends driving the industry: The industry is undergoing fundamental restructuring driven by three forces: AI integration, efficiency mandates, and retention compression [1]. The rise of AI-native SaaS is the most significant trend, with spending on AI-native applications increasing 108% year-over-year [6]. Additionally, investors have prioritized "efficient growth," centering valuation predictions on the Rule of 40 (combining revenue growth and profit margin) rather than growth alone [8].
•Geographic market distribution: North America remains the largest market, retaining a 32.85% revenue share (2025) and dominating with 46.9% of the global market in other reports [2][5]. Asia Pacific is the fastest-growing region, posting the highest regional CAGR at 24.60% [2]. By end-user industry, Banking, Financial Services, and Insurance (BFSI) led with 24.05% share, while healthcare is projected to grow at a 29.50% CAGR to 2031 [2].

Investment & Funding Trends
•Recent funding rounds and investment patterns: In 2026, the market has matured, with boards and investors prioritizing sustainable, efficient growth over rapid, capital-intensive expansion [8]. Funding is increasingly concentrated in companies demonstrating clear paths to scale without burning excessive capital [8]. The Rule of 40 has emerged as the most reliable predictor of valuation, outperforming revenue growth alone [8].
•Hot SaaS categories attracting capital: Vertical SaaS and Cybersecurity stand out as sectors offering outsized growth opportunities and commanding premium valuations [8]. AI-native SaaS is the dominant capital attractor, with spending up 108% year-over-year [6]. The BFSI sector remains the largest end-user, while healthcare is the fastest-growing niche for future investment [2].
•Average valuation multiples by company stage: Valuation multiples are now heavily dependent on the Rule of 40. Enterprise companies with ACVs above $100K retain at 118% Annual NRR and grow profitably at a median Rule of 40 of 42 [1]. Median revenue growth for private B2B SaaS has settled at 26%, while Annual NRR compressed to 101% [1].
