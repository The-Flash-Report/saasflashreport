📈 SaaS MARKET ANALYSIS - July 22, 2026

Market Overview
•The global SaaS market is currently estimated in a wide band of roughly $375 billion to $465 billion in 2026, reflecting differences in methodology across research firms; one benchmark pegs it at $465.03 billion for 2026, while another places the market near $375.6 billion. [8][10][7]
•Growth remains strong but has clearly normalized from the pandemic-era surge: forecasts cluster around 13%–18% CAGR through the early 2030s, with projections ranging from about $976.6 billion by 2031 to $1.37 trillion by 2035 depending on the source. [5][8][10]
•Enterprise software spending is still expanding rapidly, with Gartner-linked figures cited in industry reports showing double-digit growth in 2026 and software remaining one of the fastest-growing IT spend categories. [1][8]
•The strongest structural trends are AI integration, usage-based pricing, vertical SaaS specialization, and a shift from “system of record” products toward system of action workflows that automate work rather than merely store data. [3][6][7]
•Geographic concentration remains highest in North America, which continues to dominate enterprise software spend and SaaS vendor headquarters concentration, while Europe and Asia-Pacific are the main expansion markets; the source set does not provide a clean 2026 regional split, so this is an inference from market structure rather than a directly quoted statistic. [1][8]

Investment & Funding Trends
•Capital is increasingly concentrated in AI-native software companies, with one 2026 industry report stating that AI companies absorbed 61% of all VC deal value in 2025, squeezing traditional SaaS funding availability. [3]
•Private SaaS fundamentals have cooled: median growth reportedly fell to 26% in one benchmark and to 12% in another 2025/2026-oriented report, while annual NRR compressed to about 101%, signaling tougher retention and expansion dynamics. [1][3]
•For investors, the hottest categories are AI-native infrastructure, vertical SaaS, security/governance, usage-based billing and metering, and workflow automation that replaces labor rather than adding another point solution. [3][7][11]
•Valuation multiples remain bifurcated: a 2026 M&A report expects public SaaS multiples to stabilize around 5.0x–6.5x revenue, with AI-native leaders sustaining 10x+ and weaker horizontal point solutions trading at 2x–4x. [4]
•The same report pegs private SaaS venture deal multiples around 4.2x–5.2x and private SaaS M&A multiples around 3.8x–4.8x, implying disciplined pricing outside the premium AI cohort. [4]
•The practical implication for founders is that investors are paying for efficient growth, strong retention, and credible AI differentiation, not just top-line expansion. [1][4]

Industry Performance
•Public SaaS equities remain under pressure relative to the 2021 peak, with the market repricing around profitability, AI exposure, and retention quality rather than pure growth.
