Essential SaaS Tools for Startups
•CRM & Sales:
1. HubSpot CRM (Free tier for startups; Paid Suite Team at $55/agent/month) – All-in-one marketing, sales, and service platform with real-time customer records[2]. Ideal for Pre-MVP to Series A[6].
2. Pipedrive (Starting ~$14.90/user/month) – Easy-to-use sales CRM with mobile app and visual pipeline management[8]. Best for SMBs scaling sales teams[8].
3. Apollo (Free tier; Pro at $49/user/month) – Sales prospecting and automation with AI-driven lead scoring[2]. Target: B2B startups needing rapid outbound growth[2].

•Marketing Automation:
1. HubSpot Marketing (Suite Team at $55/agent/month; Professional at $115/agent/month) – Email campaigns, lead nurturing, segmentation, and A/B testing[2]. Fits startups to mid-market[2].
2. ActiveCampaign (Starting ~$15/month) – Advanced automation, customer segmentation, and social posting[2]. Ideal for small to mid-sized businesses[2].
3. Mailchimp (Free tier; Paid from $13/month) – Drag-and-drop email builder with basic automation and social posting[2]. Best for e-commerce startups and small brands[2].

•Customer Support:
1. Zendesk (Starting ~$19/agent/month; Enterprise at $169/agent/month) – Chatbots, ticket routing, SLA monitoring, and CSAT tracking[2]. Suitable for startups to large enterprises[2].
2. Intercom (Starting ~$39/agent/month; Enterprise at $17,398/year avg spend) – Conversational support with AI bots and personalized messaging[5]. Best for product-led startups[5].
3. Freshdesk (Free tier; Paid from $15/agent/month) – Simple ticketing, FAQs, and multi-channel support[2]. Ideal for pre-MVP to scaling startups[2].
