🛠️ SaaS TOOL RECOMMENDATIONS - May 20, 2026

Essential SaaS Tools for Startups

•CRM & Sales
1. HubSpot CRM — Free tier available; paid plans scale later.
- Key features: Centralized contacts, interactions, deals, tasks, and pipeline management; easy startup onboarding; strong integration ecosystem.
- Target company size: Pre-launch to growth-stage startups.
- Why it stands out: A strong free startup CRM that can grow with the team. [1]

2. Webflow — Pricing varies by plan; starter plan is available with a free Webflow.io domain, then paid tiers for custom domains and higher limits.
- Key features: No-code website building, CMS, forms, and brand-ready marketing pages.
- Target company size: Early-stage startups to scaling SMBs.
- Why it stands out: Useful for building sales-ready sites without engineering bandwidth. [2][6]

3. DealHub — Enterprise pricing not listed in the source.
- Key features: Quote-to-revenue automation, sales process acceleration, and revenue workflow management.
- Target company size: Growth-stage to enterprise sales teams.
- Why it stands out: Best fit when a startup needs stronger quote and revenue ops. [3]

•Marketing Automation
1. Mailchimp — Pricing not fully listed in the source; widely used across startup teams.
- Key features: Email campaigns, landing pages, and social ad support.
- Target company size: Small teams to mid-market.
- Why it stands out: Fastest path to simple lifecycle marketing for lean teams. [4]

2. Jotform — G2 rating 4.7/5; pricing not listed in the source.
- Key features: Modular forms, intake workflows, automation, and integrations.
- Target company size: Pre-MVP to scaling startups.
- Why it stands out: Works well for lead capture, onboarding, and operational marketing workflows. [1]

3. HubSpot Forms + CRM — Free with HubSpot CRM.
- Key features: Drag-and-drop forms, embedded/pop-up/standalone form types, automatic CRM sync.
- Target company size: Early-stage startups.
- Why it stands out: Great for marketing capture and sales follow-up in one system. [4][1]

•Customer Support
1. Zendesk — Free plan available; paid plans from $11.90/agent/month (Growth), $43/agent/month (Pro), $68/agent/month (Enterprise), billed annually.
- Key features: Customer service ticketing, support workflows, and enterprise-grade controls.
- Target company size: SMB to enterprise.
- Why it stands out: A classic support stack that scales from simple to complex environments. [2]

2. HelpKit — Pricing not fully listed; cited as a micro-SaaS example reaching $5k MRR.
- Key features: Turns Notion pages into knowledge bases.
- Target company size: Startups and small teams.
- Why it stands out: Lightweight support-doc alternative for teams already using Notion. [5]

3. Checkbox — Pricing not listed in the source.
- Key features: Automated assessments, decision-tree workflows, form building, and document automation.
- Target company size: Mid-market to enterprise
