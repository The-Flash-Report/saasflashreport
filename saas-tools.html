🛠️ SaaS TOOL RECOMMENDATIONS - June 16, 2026

Essential SaaS Tools for Startups

•CRM & Sales
- HubSpot CRM — Free starter tier; paid plans begin at $15/user/month for Starter Customer Platform. Key features: contact management, deal pipelines, email tracking, meeting scheduling, basic automation, and Breeze AI assistant for summaries and notes. Best for pre-MVP to Series A teams that want an all-in-one, easy-to-adopt CRM. [1]
- Notion CRM / Notion — Paid plans start at $5/user/month annually for Standard and $10/user/month annually for Premium. Key features: customizable databases, docs, project notes, team wikis, and broad integrations with Slack, Jira, GitHub, Google Drive, Trello, and Zapier. Best for small teams and startups that want lightweight CRM-adjacent workflows rather than a traditional sales CRM. [1]
- Jotform — Free Starter plan available; paid plans start at $34/month annually. Key features: forms, Tables, workflow automation, and separate AI Agents pricing starting at $34/month for 25 agents and 1,000 conversations. Best for pre-MVP to scaling startups that need intake, lead capture, and operational workflow automation. [1]

•Marketing Automation
- HubSpot Marketing Hub — Pricing is not fully detailed in the supplied results, but HubSpot is highlighted as a startup-friendly platform with free CRM plus AI-assisted automation via Breeze. Key features: campaign automation, contact segmentation, forms, email workflows, and sales/marketing alignment. Best for small to mid-sized startups that want a unified growth stack. [1][3]
- Mailchimp — Pricing not specified in the supplied results. Key features: email campaigns, landing pages, Facebook/Instagram ads, audience segmentation, websites, and built-in CRM. Best for early-stage startups and lean marketing teams needing a straightforward marketing platform. [3]
- Jotform — Free and paid plans starting at $34/month annually. Key features: intake forms, lead routing, automations, and AI Agents for conversational workflows. Best for small startups using forms as the front end for marketing capture and follow-up. [1]

•Customer Support
- HubSpot Service Hub — Pricing not specified in the supplied results. Key features: shared inbox, ticketing, knowledge base, automation, and Breeze AI support for summarization and task handling. Best for startups and SMBs that want support tied to CRM data. [1]
- Intercom — Pricing not specified in the supplied results. Key features: messaging-first support, automation, and strong integrations; it is also listed as a destination for PostHog data, indicating active workflow integration. Best for product-led SaaS teams with in-app support needs. [1]
- PostHog — Primarily an analytics platform, but it can route event data to Slack, HubSpot, Intercom, and webhooks. Key features: product analytics plus operational routing for support workflows. Best for technical startups that want support signals connected to product behavior. [1]

Enterprise SaaS Solutions

•Business Intelligence
- Tableau — Pricing not provided in the supplied results. Key features: enterprise dashboards, visual analytics, broad BI adoption. Best for mid-market to enterprise analytics teams.
- Power BI — Pricing not provided in the supplied results. Key features: Microsoft ecosystem integration, self-service BI, dashboards, and reporting. Best for mid-market and enterprise organizations standardize
