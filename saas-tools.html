🛠️ SaaS TOOL RECOMMENDATIONS - May 23, 2026

*Note: The sources provided are mostly roundup/list articles rather than vendor pricing pages or release notes, so pricing below is best treated as typical market pricing where not explicitly stated in the sources. I’ve prioritized tools highlighted in recent 2026 roundups and startup-focused guides.*

Essential SaaS Tools for Startups



# CRM & Sales


1. HubSpot CRM — Free tier available; paid plans scale up
- Key features: contact management, deal pipeline, tasks, activity tracking, integrations
- Best for: startups from post-launch to scaling
- Why it stands out: widely recommended as a free CRM that can grow with the company. [1]

2. Pipedrive — Typically starts around $14–$24/user/month
- Key features: visual sales pipeline, lead management, automations, reporting
- Best for: small teams and SMBs
- Why it stands out: simple, sales-first workflow for early revenue teams. [1][6]

3. Zoho CRM — Typically starts around $14/user/month
- Key features: sales automation, lead scoring, omnichannel communication, analytics
- Best for: SMBs to mid-market
- Why it stands out: strong value for teams that want broader business-suite options. [2][6]

# Marketing Automation


1. HubSpot Marketing Hub — Free starter tools; paid tiers from roughly $20+/month
- Key features: email marketing, landing pages, lead capture, CRM syncing, workflows
- Best for: startups and SMBs
- Why it stands out: pairs naturally with HubSpot CRM for an all-in-one funnel. [1]

2. Mailchimp — Free tier; paid plans vary by contacts
- Key features: email campaigns, audience segmentation, basic automations, templates
- Best for: early-stage startups and small businesses
- Why it stands out: easy to launch quickly with minimal setup. [2]

3. Brevo (Sendinblue) — Free tier; paid plans from around $9/month
- Key features: email + SMS marketing, automation, transactional email, CRM-lite tools
- Best for: budget-conscious startups and SMBs
- Why it stands out: strong value if you want multichannel marketing without enterprise cost. [2]

# Customer Support


1. Zendesk — Typically starts around $19–$55/agent/month
- Key features: ticketing, omnichannel support, help center, automations, AI-assisted workflows
- Best for: SMBs to enterprise
- Why it stands out: mature support platform with broad ecosystem and scalability. [2]

2. Intercom — Typically premium-priced; usually starts higher than basic helpdesks
- Key features: live chat, in-app messaging, AI support, proactive customer engagement
- Best for: SaaS teams focused on product-led growth
- Why it stands out: strong for onboarding, activation, and conversational support.

3. Freshdesk — Free tier available; paid plans from roughly $15/agent/month
- Key features: ticketing, automations, knowledge base, SLA management
- Best for: small teams to mid-market
- Why it stands out: good balance of cost and support functionality. [2]
