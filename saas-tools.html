🛠️ SaaS TOOL RECOMMENDATIONS - June 01, 2026

Essential SaaS Tools for Startups

•CRM & Sales
- HubSpot CRM — Free starter CRM; paid tiers scale up as teams grow. Key features: contact management, deal tracking, pipeline automation, email tools, and a broad app ecosystem. Best fit: early-stage to mid-market startups that want an easy entry point and room to expand.[1][4][5]
- Apollo.io — Free plan available; paid plans add more credits and capabilities. Key features: large contact database, outbound sequencing, prospecting, and built-in sales workflow tools. Best fit: small to mid-sized sales teams that need an all-in-one prospecting platform.[2]
- Clay — Starting at $134/month. Key features: AI-powered research, data enrichment, and 150+ provider integrations with waterfall enrichment. Best fit: growth-stage startups and revenue teams that need sophisticated prospecting and enrichment.[2]

•Marketing Automation
- Mailchimp — Pricing varies by plan; widely used for email campaigns and startup marketing workflows. Key features: email marketing, audience segmentation, templates, automation, and basic analytics. Best fit: early-stage startups and SMBs that need a simple email marketing stack.[1][5]
- Jotform — Pricing not specified in the results; positioned as modular tools you can assemble as you grow. Key features: forms, intake workflows, and automation for lead capture and operations. Best fit: pre-MVP to scaling startups that want flexible workflow building.[4]
- Zapier — Free plan with 100 tasks; paid tiers expand automation volume and app usage. Key features: no-code automation, 8,000+ app integrations, and workflow orchestration. Best fit: startups of all sizes that want to connect marketing tools without engineering work.[2][5]

•Customer Support
- Intercom — Pricing not specified in the results. Key features: customer messaging, support workflows, and AI-enabled customer communication. Best fit: startup to mid-market companies that want conversational support and in-app messaging.[1]
- HubSpot Service Hub — Pricing not specified in the results. Key features: support ticketing, shared inbox, knowledge base, and CRM-connected service workflows. Best fit: teams already using HubSpot CRM that want unified sales and support data. [Inference based on HubSpot suite positioning in the sources][1][4]
- Jotform — Pricing not specified in the results. Key features: intake forms, routing, and workflow automation that can handle lightweight support and request triage. Best fit: small teams and ops-heavy startups needing structured intake before full helpdesk adoption.[4]

Enterprise SaaS Solutions

•Business Intelligence
- Zylo — Pricing not specified in the results. Key features: SaaS discovery, spend visibility, usage analytics, and optimization. Best fit: mid-market and enterprise IT/finance teams managing a large software estate.[3]
- Torii — Pricing not specified in the results. Key features: automatic app discovery, usage tracking, shadow IT visibility, and AI-driven savings opportunities. Best fit: enterprise SaaS operations and IT teams focused on governance and cost control.[3]
- Zluri — Pricing not specified in the results. Key features: real-time insights into usage, spending, security risk, and integrations with 500+ SaaS apps. Best fit: larger organizations needing centralized SaaS intelligence and governance.[3]
