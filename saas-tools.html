Essential SaaS Tools for Startups

•CRM & Sales
- HubSpot CRM — Free core CRM; paid Sales Hub tiers scale up as teams grow. Best for pre-launch to SMB startups that need contact management, pipeline tracking, tasks, and a free entry point with room to scale.[1]
- Pipedrive — Typically priced starting around a low-cost per-seat monthly plan; strongest for small sales teams that want visual pipeline management, deal tracking, and sales automation. Recent market roundups continue to position it as a practical startup sales tool, though the provided results do not include full pricing details.[8]
- Close — Usually positioned as a mid-market sales CRM with calling, emailing, and workflow automation for fast-moving sales teams. The search results do not include current pricing, so verify the live plan page before purchase.[8]

•Marketing Automation
- Mailchimp — Free tier available; paid plans increase with audience size. Best for early-stage startups and SMBs needing email campaigns, landing pages, and social ads in one platform; it is highlighted in a 2026 startup tools roundup.[4]
- HubSpot Marketing Hub — Entry-level tools are often bundled with HubSpot’s CRM ecosystem; suited to startup through mid-market teams that want forms, email, automation, and CRM alignment. HubSpot CRM is explicitly recommended for startups in the provided results.[1]
- Jotform — Has a free tier and paid automation plans; best for pre-MVP to scaling startups needing forms, workflows, and lightweight marketing automation/integrations.[1]

•Customer Support
- Intercom — Usually priced by seats and usage; best for growth-stage startups to mid-market teams that want live chat, AI support, and in-app messaging. No direct pricing appears in the provided results, so confirm current plans on the vendor site.
- Zendesk — Commonly used by SMB to enterprise teams for ticketing, omnichannel support, and analytics. The search results do not provide live pricing, so treat pricing as vendor-quote dependent.
- Freshdesk — Often offers a free or low-cost entry tier and scales for small to mid-sized support teams that need ticketing, self-service, and automations. Pricing is not listed in the provided results.
