NetBox Labs Secures $35M Series B to Accelerate Network Management Innovation

📊 SaaS METRICS SNAPSHOT - July 15, 2025

Market Performance
• SaaS stock performance and valuation multiples: While specific stock prices fluctuate daily, the SaaS sector continues to see moderate valuation multiples reflecting steady but slowing growth. Median B2B SaaS growth rates have declined for three consecutive years, with the top quartile growth rate dropping from 60% in 2023 to 50% in 2024, indicating a more cautious market valuation environment[1][2].
• Recent funding round sizes and trends: Funding rounds remain robust but show a trend toward more selective investment, with VC-backed SaaS companies investing heavily in sales and marketing (44% of revenue) compared to PE-backed firms (33%), reflecting a focus on growth despite slowing expansion[2].
• ARR growth rates trending in the market: Median actual ARR growth rate was 26% in 2024, down from previous years, while companies plan for a 35% growth rate in 2025, signaling optimism but also a gap between expectations and reality[1][2].

Key Industry Numbers
• Average SaaS pricing model trends: Usage-based pricing models are gaining traction, but per-seat pricing remains dominant in many segments. Expansion ARR (revenue from existing customers) now represents 40% of total new ARR, increasing to over 50% in companies with ARR above $50M, indicating a shift toward monetizing existing customers more effectively[2].
• Customer acquisition cost (CAC) benchmarks by segment: New customer CAC ratios increased by 14% in 2024, reflecting higher sales and marketing expenses, especially in VC-backed companies. However, blended CAC ratios decreased by 10% due to a higher contribution of expansion ARR to new ARR[2].
• Churn rate industry averages: Net Revenue Retention (NRR) stands at 101%, showing that while churn is being managed, retaining and expanding existing customers is becoming more challenging as companies rely more on expansion ARR[2].
